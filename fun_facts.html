What is life if not an adventure?

My life is not just coding and university coursework. In fact, the vast majority of events that shaped me as an individual happened outside of classroom. If you are reading this section right now, it probably means that my professional self has interested you. Thank you for that! In this section, I would like to share some of the dearest experiences to my heart that have shaped me as an individual, taught me self-discipline and perseverance, and shown that communication is the most important skill one must have.

Before coming to the US, I spent two years in the northeast of Italy, at UWC Adriatic, one of the United World College movement's institutions. This movement aspires to make education a force to unite people, nations and cultures for peace and a sustainable future. To do that, UWC Adriatic brings 180 students from more than 90 countries to pursue the rigorous International Baccalaureate (IB) Diploma.

Living at this residential school was demanding yet truly transformational. Just think about it, you spend two years with 180 students with different in any way possible backgrounds under the same roof on the beautiful coast of the Adriatic sea. What an immense learning opportunity! It were the small quarrels with fellow students from a plethora of backgrounds that taught me patience and key conflict resolution skills; it were the long study sessions with my friends that created the unbreakable bonds between us and taught me that people are one's main asset.
